The Feminine Mystique - 1963

This book, written by Betty Friedan, encouraged many women to re-examine their socital roles.

Freedom House Strike - 1964

During the Civil Rights Movement, many women were forced to serve the male activists of the time. At one civil rights headquarters, the women went on strike and refused to do any cooking or cleaning.

Executive Order 11375 signed into law - 1967

Signed by President Johnson, this order banned sex-based discrimination in all government workplaces.

Radical Women protest the Miss America Pagent - 1968

A feminist group staged a protest in which they threw away bras, girdles, curlers, false eyelashes, and wigs.

Women's International Terrorist Conspiracy From Hell (WITCH) formed - 1968

The group help many protests for women's rights, including the firing of a feminist teacher and the treatment of women office workers.

The Political Economy of Women's Liberation - 1969

Author Margaret Benson compared women to servents, as they worked just as hard (if not harder) than their male counterparts, but since the work they did was unpaid, it was considered to have less value.

Roe v. Wade - 1973

This court case gave women the right to an abortion if the pregnancy was still within the first trimester.

Our Bodies, Ourselves - 1973

This book contained information for women regarding their health and the female reproductive system. Such topics were considered taboo or inapropriate at the time, and as a result, many women knew nothing about their own bodies.

Antional Organization for Women (NOW) reaches 70,000 members - 1977

NOW advocated for women's rights, particularly the Equal Rights Amendment.

Divorces Double - 1980

The divorce rates in the US have doubled since 1960. Now, more women feel comfortable leaving an unhappy marriage instead of staying to please their husbands.
